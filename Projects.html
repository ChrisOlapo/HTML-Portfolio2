Afrobeats in Lockdown

During the first year of lockdown, Nigeria’s big 3 heavy hitters (Burna Boy, Davido & Wizkid) all gave phenomenal albums and took the world by storm. Burna Boy linked up with Diddy and give us soulful tracks such as 23 and Real Life. Davido shows us a different side of him with A Better time as he dips his toe on Amapiano song Heaven and soulful song Fem. Wizkid came and finished the year off strong with songs of the year Ginger and Essence from his Made in Lagos classic.

From running a Google trend search on their global reach it easy to see that Burna Boy dominated by having a stronger impact which was also helped by featuring on the South African song “Jerusalema”. Davido has a greater impact across the African continent and also in the country of Dubai, where he would be asked to close out the FIFA World cup 2 years later after the albulm release. With the addictive melodic tones of Essence it easy to see how Wizkid reached Russian territories.

Blood Sisters

In 2021 Netflix joined with Nollywood to produce one of the most talked about pieces of content on the internet – Blood Sisters. By conducting social media analysis Paul Nnakwe and Ebenezer Akin provided valuable insights into the audience's reactions, opinions, and discussions surrounding the movie. By understanding the positive influence the film resonated with viewers and it allows the opportunity of the audience to engage across various social media platforms.

By analysing user-generated content, reviews, and hashtags related to the film, one can gather valuable feedback and leverage it to enhance the film's visibility and engagement. These social media conversations it helps to promote the film via word of mouth which is advantageous in an increasing virtual world.
